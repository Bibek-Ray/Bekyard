A Short story on Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

Author: Bibek Ray   |   Date: 21st August 2024

Meta.ai recently launched their latest model Llama 3.1, which can generate realistic images for free!
It was fascinating to see how realistic the images it was able to create, which made me ponder upon the question, "How does image generation work?"
So I decided to spend a few hours learning some of the very basics of image generation. During my exploration, I stumbled upon Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), a key concept in generative AI. To make it simpler and more fun to understand, I came up with an analogy involving two friends, Garry and Danny. Let me share it with you!

The Tale of Garry and Danny: Understanding GANs:
Meet the friends: Garry (Generator):

  • Garry is the storyteller. His job is to create stories (images) that sound real.
  • Initially, Garry’s stories are terrible and obviously fake.
Danny (Discriminator):
  • Danny is the critic. His job is to identify which stories (images) are real and which are fake.
  • Initially, Danny is bad at distinguishing fake stories from real ones.
The Storybook (Image Dataset):
  • The stories (images) that Danny uses to learn are based on a storybook filled with true stories (real images).
  • This storybook helps Danny recognize the details that make a story real.


Comic book representation of GANs

The Debate:

Initially, both the dumb friends start debating, with,

  • Garry telling a fake story, and Danny trying to catch it.
  • Through feedback and improvement, Garry makes his stories more believable, and Danny becomes better at spotting fakes.
Outcome:
After many rounds of this back-and-forth, Garry becomes a master storyteller, and Danny becomes an expert critic.
Summary:
Both start off as novices but improve each other through constant interaction.
By the end, Garry can create highly realistic stories (images), and Danny can accurately identify real and fake stories (images).
